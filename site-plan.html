Nigerian Arts & Culture Portal

Site Plan Document

Site Name

Nigerian Arts & Culture Portal

This name was selected to clearly communicate the focus on Nigerian art and cultural heritage. The term "Portal" suggests a comprehensive gateway that connects visitors to various aspects of Nigerian artistic expression and cultural elements. This straightforward and descriptive name will help both local and international users immediately understand the site's purpose and content focus.

Optional domain availability: nigerianartsandculture.com or nacp.org

Site Purpose

The Nigerian Arts & Culture Portal serves as a comprehensive digital platform dedicated to celebrating, preserving, and promoting Nigeria's rich artistic and cultural heritage. The site's core purposes include:

  • Providing a centralized online resource for exploring diverse Nigerian artistic expressions, including visual arts, music, literature, and film
  • Creating a showcase for both established and emerging Nigerian artists to gain visibility
  • Serving as an educational resource about Nigerian cultural traditions, artistic movements, and contemporary developments
  • Connecting visitors with cultural events, exhibitions, and performances across Nigeria and internationally
  • Building community through interactive features that allow visitors to engage with content and connect with artists

Through these services, the portal aims to make Nigerian arts and culture more accessible to both local and global audiences while supporting the promotion and preservation of Nigeria's cultural heritage.

Scenarios

Scenario 1

"I'm a researcher studying West African art forms and need to find information about traditional Nigerian sculpture techniques and their contemporary applications. Where can I find comprehensive information about notable Nigerian sculptors and their works?"

This scenario represents academic researchers and art enthusiasts seeking in-depth information about specific Nigerian art forms.

Scenario 2

"I'm planning a trip to Lagos and want to experience authentic Nigerian cultural events during my visit. How can I find a calendar of upcoming exhibitions, music performances, and cultural festivals?"

This scenario represents international tourists who want to engage with Nigerian cultural activities during their visit.

Scenario 3

"I'm a Nigerian artist living abroad and want to connect with the local art scene back home. How can I find information about galleries, artist residencies, and submission opportunities for Nigerian artists?"

This scenario represents members of the Nigerian diaspora seeking to maintain connections with their cultural roots and artistic communities.

Color Applications

  • Primary Color (Deep Green #16613D): Main navigation, headers, footer, and primary buttons. Represents Nigeria's lush vegetation and is part of the national color palette.
  • Secondary Color (Gold #F7C64A): Sub-headings, highlights, secondary buttons, and background accents. Represents the vibrant energy of Nigerian culture and artistic expression.
  • Accent Color (Red #E63946): Call-to-action elements, important notifications, and special feature highlights. Adds energy and draws attention to key elements.
  • Text Dark (#333333): Primary text color for optimal readability.
  • Text Light (#FFFFFF): Text on dark backgrounds for proper contrast.
  • Background Light (#F4F4F4): Page backgrounds to enhance readability and content focus.
